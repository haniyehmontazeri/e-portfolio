In today's life, using a car is inevitable. Therefore, in this context, car and driver safety is one of the most important concerns in the car industry. One of the most important factors in driving accidents is the driver's fatigue and drowsiness. In the literal words drowsiness is a tired state between sleeping and being awake, which can be dangerous during performing tasks that require concentration, such as driving a vehicle. Fig. 1 indicates the statistic of road accident in Malaysia from the year 2005 to 2009 provided by MIROS (Malaysia Institute of Road Safety). In 2006, the number of car and taxi accident was about 400,000 cases. It keeps increasing every year until it reaches nearly 500,000 cases in 2009. In this century by the development of technology, detecting and preventing drowsiness during driving on the road is a challenge which is required to be developed in order to reduce the rate of accidents. (1) A Driver Drowsiness System is designed to detect driver's eye directly in order to recognize fatigue. After that if the system determine that driver is sleepy or drowsy, it will play alarm to alert the driver (2).


Blinking is a semi-autonomic rapid function of closing eyelid. In this project we use face-recognition library and facial landmark detector in two different sections. In the first section which is called face detection, we try to recognize the driver's face in a rectangle in order to limit the area that we want to work with. Then, after we got the face, it's time to point inside the rectangle especially the eyes. Eye Aspect Ratio is a method that use for the close or open state of the eyes. In this method, we use different land marks to distinguish the state of the eyes. These landmarks are used to calculate the distance between vertical eye landmarks and the horizontal one. The result will show when the eye is open because the eye aspect ratio will rapidly decrease to zero during a blink. To calculate the EAR we used the following formula (3).
